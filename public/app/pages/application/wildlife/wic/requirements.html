REVISED PCSD Administrative Order No. 12 - Series of 2011

Guidelines for the Regulationand Monitoring of Catching, Culture, Trade, Transport and Export of Reef-Fish-For-Food In Palawan

Wildlife Import Certification – means a privileged permit issued to any individual, acadamic, reseasch institution or organization to capture/harvest and transport wildlife species from the natural habitat for noncommercial scientific or educational undertaking;

Section 2.3

Importation of All Wildlife Species, their By-products and Derivatives - if the importation of wildlife, their by-products and derivatives, is intended to have Palawan as its point of destination, there shall be required CITES/Non-CITES Import Permit from the PCSD.

Import permit – refers to a permit authorizing an individual to bring in wildlife from another country;

CITES Import Permit – permit authorizing an individual to bring into the Philippines wildlife listed under CITES Appendices, including its by-products or derivatives, from oilier countries;

Section 23.1

Requirements

  • Duly accomplished application form;
  • CITES/Non-CITES Export Permit/Certification of Origin from exporting country;
  • For terrestrial wildlife, Phytosanitary Certificate (for plants) or Veterinary Quarantine Certificate (for animals) from concerned government office of the country of origin; For aquatic wildlife, Health/Veterinary certificate issued by the competent government agency of the country of origin;
  • Document Attached
  • Payment of Fees;

Section 23.2

Procedure

All applications under this Section, together with their corresponding requirements, shall be submitted to the PCSDS;

The PCSDS shall immediately inspect or verify the wildlife subject of the application using all available documents that contain their descriptions; and

The CITES/Non-CITES Import Permit shall be issued by the PCSDS Executive Director upon evaluation of the recommendation of the technical staff.
